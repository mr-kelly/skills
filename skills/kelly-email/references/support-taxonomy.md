# Support Taxonomy

Use this reference to classify inbound support emails and prepare consistent replies.

## Categories

- `bug_report`: Something is broken or not behaving as expected.
- `how_to`: Customer asks how to use a feature or complete a workflow.
- `billing`: Invoice, payment, subscription, refund, cancellation, receipt, or tax question.
- `account_access`: Login, password, SSO, permission, verification, lockout, or ownership issue.
- `sales_or_upgrade`: Pricing, trial, plan comparison, procurement, demo, or upgrade.
- `feature_request`: Customer asks for a new feature, integration, export, or customization.
- `data_privacy_security`: Data deletion/export, privacy, security review, vulnerability, compliance, or DPA.
- `complaint`: Frustration, repeated failure, poor experience, or churn risk.
- `partnership`: Affiliate, integration partner, reseller, influencer, community, or co-marketing.
- `course_feedback`: Lesson feedback, homework submissions, learning blockers, ratings, or course/product education survey answers.
- `spam_or_irrelevant`: Cold pitch, mass marketing, unrelated newsletter, or obvious automated noise.
- `other`: Needs manual interpretation.

## Priority Signals

Raise priority for:

- Paid customer is blocked from using the product.
- The sender has followed up more than once.
- The thread mentions churn, cancellation, refund, chargeback, legal action, security, or privacy.
- The message involves money: invoices, receipts, payments, charges, renewals, bank transactions, taxes, payroll, domain registration/renewal, or explicit amounts.
- A concrete deadline is today or tomorrow.
- The sender is a known high-value customer, partner, investor, or VIP.

Lower priority for:

- Generic cold outreach.
- Automated marketing emails.
- Low-context feature requests without current impact.
- Messages already answered by a recent reply in the thread.

## Reply Quality Bar

Good support replies:

- Acknowledge the customer's specific issue.
- Give the answer or next step in the first few sentences.
- Ask for only the minimum missing information.
- Avoid internal jargon.
- Do not blame the customer.
- Include a realistic timeline only when verified.
- End with a clear next action.
- Stay compact. Default to 60-180 words and short paragraphs.
- Cut setup phrases, repeated context, and generic enthusiasm.
- Include a short quoted excerpt when replying to a customer thread, but trim it aggressively.

## Copy Compression Checklist

Before presenting a draft:

- Can the first sentence carry the main answer?
- Can any paragraph become one line?
- Can a long explanation become 3-5 bullets?
- Are all links/contact methods necessary?
- Is there exactly one obvious next step?

## Internal Notes

Internal notes should state:

- Evidence used from the thread or related context.
- Risk level: `low`, `medium`, or `high`.
- Missing information.
- Recommended owner when escalation is needed.

## Cleanup Reporting

For auto-cleaned mail, report useful audit detail:

- Category counts.
- Sender/domain.
- Short subject.
- Reason for cleanup.

Avoid dumping bodies. The user needs to know what disappeared from unread, not reread the messages.
